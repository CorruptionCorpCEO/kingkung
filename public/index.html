Game Rules

Chip Selection and Movement:

  • Players take turns moving their chips, which can move one space in any direction (vertically, horizontally, or diagonally) similar to a king in chess.
  • The board in King Kung wraps around, allowing chips to be moved past one side of the board and spit out the other.
  • A chip can only move to a space if it is not occupied by another chip.
  • On their turn, a player clicks on one of their chips to see potential moves, highlighted with semi-transparent circles.
  • Clicking on a highlighted potential move space moves the chip to that space.

Chip Swapping Mechanic:

If a chip is adjacent to two enemy chips vertically or horizontally, it can swap places with one of those enemy chips. Swapping chips cannot be undone immediately; players cannot move back to the original position if it would undo a swap.

Winning the Game:

A player wins by lining up all four of their chips in a vertical, horizontal or diagonal line. Upon winning, a notification displays the winner, the game is reset, and the winner's score is incremented.
